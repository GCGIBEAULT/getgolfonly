About GolfSolo

GolfSolo is built for golfers who want to record their round without distractions—and get right back to the next shot. No internet required.

No accounts. No ads. No pop-ups. No tracking. No upsells. Just golf.

Every round starts instantly. Every hole takes seconds. Your scorecard is always visible, always simple, always yours. GolfSolo is the digital pencil — nothing more, nothing less.

Whether you're walking nine at sunset or grinding through 18 on the weekend, enter your stats on GolfSolo. Put your phone back in your pocket. Stay in the moment.

It isn't here to teach shot shapes or mechanics. It's here to show you where you lost strokes today — so you know what to work on tomorrow.

And as every golfer knows, tomorrow never gets here fast enough.

Why I Built GolfSolo

Last year, I threw away my last paper scorecard and started building GolfSolo.

I didn't count them, but over the years I threw away a lot of scorecards.

It's 2026. I figured it was time to modernize something that hadn't fundamentally changed in 165 years.

Tracking your score isn't golf tradition. It's simply a way of recording your round. It needed to evolve, and I decided to give it a shove in that direction.

Like the computer or phone you're reading this on, golf has embraced modern technology in nearly every part of the game. Your clubs probably aren't 100 years old—although I do own a set that are.

GolfSolo was never meant to reinvent golf. It was meant to replace a paper scorecard with something that simply works better.

One page. Almost no scrolling. The information that matters. Your complete golf history, available whenever you want it.

I've played this game my entire life. I built the scorekeeping app I always wanted. GolfSolo asks for only a few seconds after each hole. The rest of the round belongs to you. I hope you enjoy using GolfSolo as much as I enjoyed building it.

— Gregg Gibeault
